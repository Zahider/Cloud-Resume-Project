ReviewSensAI - Llama 3 Hackathon by Meta/Together.AI

Why We Joined the Llama 3 Hackathon


Our team, proudly nerdy and ever-ready to embrace a challenge, dove into the Llama 3 hackathon by Meta and Together.ai with gusto. We were driven by a thirst for new skills and a desire to tackle something outside our usual comfort zone. None of us had ever wrangled with Together.ai, built a Chrome extension, or faced the exhilarating pressure of a 48-hour deadline. Little did we know, due to some unforeseen calamities, we’d end up with less than 24 hours. Nothing like a little chaos to spice up a hackathon, right?

How We Created Review Sensai


With the clock ticking, we divided our tasks based on our superpowers: I took on project management with an agile and test-driven approach, Dani A. became our API whisperer, and Aiman M. wielded AI like a pro. Our project, Review SensAI, is an AI sentiment analysis extension designed to read through product reviews and determine if a product is worth buying. It analyzes all available reviews, assigns a score out of 100, and highlights notable mentions that frequently appear.

We used TypeScript, React, Vite, and Together.ai to bring this vision to life—because what’s a hackathon without a few buzzwords? Despite our best efforts, a time crunch and AI credit shortages meant we couldn’t integrate our AI model fully. Instead, we hardcoded an example into our extension, proving that even under duress, we could still deliver something cool. Challenges? Oh, we had plenty, but we managed to turn chaos into a functional prototype as you can see above. Check out my GitHub repository below for more details on our development journey.

The Outcome and Impact


In the end, despite having only half the time we’d originally planned, we were over the moon with Review Sensai. We may not have nailed the AI model integration, but our hardcoded example was enough to show off our concept's potential. The judges and participants were impressed, and we got some solid positive feedback. Looking ahead, if we decide to give this project another whirl, we’re planning to add features that provide businesses with insights, not just make online shoppers’ lives easier. After all, if you’re going to go through a hackathon’s rollercoaster, you might as well come out with something that can truly make a difference!
